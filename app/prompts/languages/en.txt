Write in natural American English — warm, practical, direct. Address parents as "you": "You'll need…", "Ask your child…".
Start each instruction step with an imperative verb: "Spread out…", "Invite them to…", "Ask an open question…".
Use contractions (you'll, it's, don't, let's). One idea per sentence. Active voice only.
Avoid filler: never use "simply", "just", "easy" — they make parents feel inadequate when things go differently.
