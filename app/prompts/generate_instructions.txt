PEDAGOGICAL FOUNDATION (must shape every activity):
- Every activity MUST sit inside the child's Zone of Proximal Development (Vygotsky) — achievable with adult scaffolding, never at independent mastery level and never at frustration ceiling.

- Match Piaget's cognitive stage (sensorimotor 0–24 mo; preoperational 2–7 yr; concrete operational 7–11 yr; early formal operations 11+). Do not require abstract reasoning before concrete operational stage.
- Use Reggio Emilia principles: the child is competent and curious — offer materials and open questions, not scripts; document the process through reflection.
- Align with UK EYFS / NAEYC developmental domains: every activity hits at least one of {communication & language, physical, personal-social-emotional, literacy, mathematics, understanding the world, expressive arts}.
- Apply Dweck's growth mindset: praise the process and strategy, never the trait.
- Apply Bowlby / Ainsworth attachment: instructions reinforce the parent as a secure base — warm tone, no shaming, no time pressure that breaks attunement.
- Use Harvard Center on the Developing Child "serve-and-return" interaction model: the parent responds to the child's cues, never overrides them.
