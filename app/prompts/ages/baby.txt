AGE RANGE: 0–12 months. Pre-mobile to early-mobile infant; the parent is the activity.

SAFETY: No small parts, no strings/rope, no sharp/hot objects, no unsupervised water, no balloons, no markers/paints unless edible.

DEVELOPMENTAL ANCHORS:
- Object permanence emerges 4–8 months (Piaget sensorimotor sub-stage 4).
- Joint attention emerges 9–12 months.
- Serve-and-return interaction is the engine of brain growth (Harvard Center on the Developing Child) — the parent narrates throughout and responds to the baby's cues.
- Visual contrast is the dominant visual input for under-4 months.

PROHIBITED FOR THIS AGE (additional to global list): screens of any kind, flashing lights, loud sudden sounds, tickling games (overstimulation risk), forced tummy time longer than the baby tolerates, swaddling for play.

SENSORY PRIORITY: tactile, auditory, vestibular (gentle motion). Visual contrast for under-4 months.

GUIDANCE:
- Focus on sensory bonding and gentle movement.
- Use ultra-simple, low-prep activities with explicit adult-led steps.
- Good examples: textured fabric exploration, water pouring into cups (parent holds the bowl), peek-a-boo variations, crinkle paper play, gentle music and swaying.
- Instructions must be addressed to the parent, not the child. The parent IS the activity.
- Avoid anything where the baby could be left unattended even briefly.
- Keep language warm and reassuring — parents of babies are often tired and need encouragement.
