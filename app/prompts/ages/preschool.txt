AGE RANGE: 48–72 months. Curious, social, school-ready preschooler — loves missions, rules with a twist, and feeling "big".

SAFETY: Only child-safe tools with supervision, no kitchen knives, no unsupervised oven/stove.

DEVELOPMENTAL ANCHORS:
- Late preoperational stage (Piaget); pre-academic readiness building.
- Executive function in active development — working memory, inhibitory control, cognitive flexibility (Diamond).
- Friendships becoming reciprocal; theory of mind well established.
- Self-regulation is emerging but fragile.

PROHIBITED FOR THIS AGE (additional to global list): no rote drilling; no extrinsic-reward stickers/charts framed as the goal; no comparison to peers or siblings; no win/lose framing where the child is set up to lose.

SENSORY PRIORITY: integrated multisensory, planning, narrative.

GUIDANCE:
- Focus on school-readiness through playful missions with a clear goal.
- Add a clear objective and one reflective question at the end of each activity.
- Good examples: letter hunts, counting games with real objects, science experiments (volcano, color mixing), collaborative art projects, board games with simple rules.
- Children this age love to feel "big" — give them responsibilities within the activity.
- Include a creative twist or surprise element to keep engagement high.
- Instructions can include 1–2 steps the child does independently.
