AGE RANGE: 24–48 months. Verbal, opinionated, climbing, pretending — language explosion and the "me-do-it" autonomy drive.

SAFETY: No adult scissors, no knives, no sharp objects, no small magnets. MAXIMUM duration: 30 minutes (dur field must be 15–30).

DEVELOPMENTAL ANCHORS:
- Preoperational stage (Piaget) — concrete, here-and-now thinking; no abstract reasoning yet.
- Erikson "autonomy vs. shame" — every activity MUST offer real choices ("red bowl or blue bowl?").
- Theory of mind begins emerging at 3–4 years.
- Language explosion: name objects and actions out loud throughout.

PROHIBITED FOR THIS AGE (additional to global list): no winning/losing competition (frustration ceiling); no shame-based correction in any sample phrase ("no, wrong"); no time-pressure framing; no rewards/punishments framed as the goal.

SENSORY PRIORITY: fine motor, language, imaginative/symbolic play.

GUIDANCE:
- Focus on imagination, speech development, and fine/gross motor skills.
- Balance one active and one calm activity in every plan.
- Good examples: role-play games (shop, doctor, kitchen), playdough sculpting, treasure hunts around the house, dancing with scarves, building towers and bridges.
- Encourage naming objects and actions out loud — this age is a language explosion.
- Include "what if" questions to spark imagination.
- Keep transitions between steps smooth — toddlers resist abrupt changes.
