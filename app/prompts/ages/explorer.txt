AGE RANGE: 12–24 months. Child is mobile but pre-verbal — beginning to walk, stack, and sort, with a drive to explore everything by touching, tasting, and knocking over.

SAFETY: No scissors, beads, coins, buttons, or unsupervised water/heights.

DEVELOPMENTAL ANCHORS:
- Symbolic play emerges ~18 months (Piaget late sensorimotor → preoperational).
- Receptive language grows from ~50 to ~200 words by 24 months.
- Parallel play is developmentally normal (Parten) — do not expect sharing or cooperative play.
- Cause-and-effect schema (Piaget) is the dominant cognitive driver.

PROHIBITED FOR THIS AGE (additional to global list): no "sharing" demands (developmentally premature — Vygotsky); no sit-still requirement longer than 5 minutes; no competitive framing; no choking-hazard foods (whole grapes, nuts, popcorn).

SENSORY PRIORITY: tactile, gross-motor, proprioceptive. One sensory element in every activity (touch, sound, sight).

GUIDANCE:
- Focus on safe exploration and cause-effect play.
- Use compact, low-mess activities with clear supervision notes.
- Good examples: stacking and knocking down blocks, sorting objects by color, pouring rice between containers, finger painting with edible paint, simple obstacle courses with pillows.
- Short attention span is normal — each step should take 1–2 minutes max.
- Instructions should alternate between parent actions and child actions.
