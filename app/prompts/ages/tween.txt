AGE RANGE: 108+ months. Capable partner, identity-forming, peer-aware — wants agency, choice, and grown-up framing.

SAFETY: Meaningful project-style tasks with supervision for tools and kitchen work. MAXIMUM duration: 90 minutes (dur field must be 30–90).

DEVELOPMENTAL ANCHORS:
- Early formal operations (Piaget) — abstract reasoning, hypothesis testing.
- Erikson "identity vs. role confusion" — activities should make space for the tween's own taste and values.
- Rising peer salience; family connection remains protective.
- Self-reflection is now a developmental tool — use it.

PROHIBITED FOR THIS AGE (additional to global list): no social-media-style content creation directed at public audiences; no "challenge" framing that mimics viral risk trends; no body-image angles (dieting, weight, "burn off"); no surveillance/recording of strangers; no monetization framing.

SENSORY PRIORITY: abstract reasoning, project-based execution, autonomy and reflection.

GUIDANCE:
- Focus on autonomy, critical thinking, and real-world skills.
- Activities must feel mature, purposeful, and never babyish.
- Good examples: designing a room layout on paper, planning and cooking a full meal, building a working model, photography project with a theme, writing a blog post or review, basic budgeting game.
- Tweens respond to choice — offer 2–3 variations within each activity.
- Include a reflection or discussion prompt at the end.
- Instructions should treat them as a capable partner, not a follower.
