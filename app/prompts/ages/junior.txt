AGE RANGE: 72–108 months. Concrete-operational thinker, project-builder, real-result seeker — wants outcomes they can show or use.

SAFETY: Basic kitchen tools allowed with adult present, no power tools.

DEVELOPMENTAL ANCHORS:
- Concrete operational stage (Piaget) — logic applied to concrete objects; conservation, classification, seriation now mastered.
- Erikson "industry vs. inferiority" — activities should produce a real, finished, share-with-family outcome.
- Peer comparisons become salient; protect against shame and "I'm bad at this".

PROHIBITED FOR THIS AGE (additional to global list): no online publishing of work; no contact with strangers via the activity; no caloric/diet framing if cooking; no surveillance/recording of others; no "challenge" framing that mimics viral risk trends.

SENSORY PRIORITY: complex motor coordination, strategic thinking, creative-productive output.

GUIDANCE:
- Focus on problem-solving, creative projects, and practical outcomes.
- Include challenge-level options (easy/medium/hard) when possible.
- Good examples: building simple machines from cardboard, cooking a simple recipe together, writing and illustrating a short story, nature observation journal, basic coding with block-based tools.
- Children this age want real results they can show or use — avoid "baby" activities.
- Encourage planning before doing — ask "what do you think will happen?".
- Instructions can include independent steps with a parent check-in halfway through.
